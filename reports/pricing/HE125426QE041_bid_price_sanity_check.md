## Pricing Realism Notes

**Contract type:** Firm Fixed Price (FFP). All costs must be embedded in unit prices.

**Structural observations:**

- **5-year FFP with option to extend.** Unit prices must absorb inflation, supply cost escalation, and labor increases over the full performance period.
- **5 service types per period.** Ant/Roach, Field/Turf, Termite, Wasp/Bee, Rodent/Small Animal — each CLIN must include all labor, materials, travel, callbacks, and contingency for that scope.
- **School environment.** DoDEA Fort Campbell serves ~4,000 students across 6 schools. Scheduling, chemical safety, staff coordination, and possible after-hours/weekend execution add labor overhead beyond basic field-time estimates.
- **Military installation access.** Fort Campbell base access requires badge/escort coordination, personnel vetting, and security compliance. Build mobilization time and overhead into per-visit cost assumptions.
- **Market range benchmark:** Similar federal awards range from $1,896,101 to $3,908,553 (median $2,192,913). These are full-contract award totals. Divide by scope and period count to calibrate per-year assumptions.

## Underpricing Risks

- **Callbacks not priced in.** FFP means re-treatment is at no additional cost to the Government. Each CLIN unit price must absorb callback frequency for that service type.
- **Access / scheduling overhead omitted.** Base entry delays, escort time, and school-day coordination add real time cost. Field-only quotes from subs may not capture this.
- **Material escalation over option years.** Pesticides, supplies, and equipment costs can rise over a 5-year contract. Fixed unit prices may compress margin in later option years.
- **Emergency / after-hours scope unclear.** Confirm whether the PWS requires out-of-hours response and whether it is included in base CLINs or separately priced.
- **Sub quote obtained too late.** If subcontractor pricing is gathered informally or at the last minute, unit prices may not reflect actual execution cost. Get quotes early.

## Overpricing Risks

- **Active small business competition.** Multiple established vendors have won similar federal pest control awards. Pricing well above the market median risks non-competitive standing.
- **Small business set-aside pool narrows field but not pricing.** Competitors are all small businesses, but pest control is a mature small-business market with capable, price-competitive vendors.
- **Unbalanced CLIN structure.** Stacking excessive contingency into one CLIN while underpricing another can trigger an evaluation notice or disqualification for unbalanced pricing.
