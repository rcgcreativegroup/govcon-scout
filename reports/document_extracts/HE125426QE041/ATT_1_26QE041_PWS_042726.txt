1.0 Introduction – Section 
1.1 Background - Subsection 
The Department of War Education Activity (DoWEA), as one of only two Federally operated 
school systems, is responsible for planning, directing, coordinating, and managing 
prekindergarten through 12th grade educational programs on behalf of the Department of War 
(DoD). DoWEA is globally positioned, operating 165 brick and mortar schools in eight districts 
located in seven states, 11 foreign countries, Guam, and Puerto Rico. DoWEA employs over 
8,000 educators who serve more than 72,000 children. DoWEA also has a fully accredited virtual 
high school located in Virginia with teaching hubs in Europe and the Pacific. 
DoWEA Fort Campbell proudly serves approximately 4,000 students across six schools, offering 
a comprehensive educational experience from Universal Preschool through 12
th grade. Nestled 
within the home of the legendary 101st Airborne Division, the Screaming Eagles, our district is 
honored to support the families of this distinguished military community. The campus spans 
nearly 280 acres and includes close to 1 million square feet of learning space across multiple 
facilities. Fort Campbell is also home to DoWEA’s first and only turf athletic field, 
complemented by a newly completed baseball and softball complex, underscoring our 
commitment to excellence in both academics and extracurricular activities. 
1.2 Scope – Subsection 
The Department of War Education Activity (DoWEA) Fort Campbell Community School 
District requires comprehensive Integrated Pest Management and Critter Control (IPMCC) 
services to maintain a safe, healthy, and sanitary environment for students, faculty, and staff. The 
contractor shall provide all necessary management, labor, tools, general and specialized 
equipment, licenses, permits, bonds, and transportation to perform pest control services in 
accordance with this Performance Work Statement (PWS) and the Pest Management Plan 
(PMP). 
Services shall include the prevention, monitoring, and control of common nuisance pests such as 
flies, ants, spiders, cockroaches, beetles, rodents, and other insects. In addition, the contractor 
shall be capable of responding on short notice to wildlife intrusions involving snakes, skunks, 
raccoons, groundhogs, and similar animals that pose immediate health or safety risks. Timely 
response to such incidents is critical to ensure uninterrupted school operations and the protection 
of occupants. Any traps or control methods used must be safe for environments with children 
present, and appropriate precautions and coordination with school staff must be taken prior to 
deployment to ensure safety and minimize disruption.

2.0 Requirement Objectives – Section 
The objective of this requirement is to ensure comprehensive and warranty-backed pest control 
services are provided across all DoWEA Fort Campbell properties in accordance with this 
Performance Work Statement (PWS). The contractor shall deliver a complete and effective 
solution that meets all specified requirements and objectives. Each pest treatment shall carry a 
minimum warranty period of 90 days, while termite treatments shall be warrantied for a 
minimum of three years. If pest activity reoccurs during the applicable warranty period, the 
contractor shall re-perform the necessary treatment(s) at no additional cost to the government. 
All treatments shall include coverage of the full interior and exterior boundaries of DoWEA 
properties. Additionally, the government reserves the right to incorporate the contractor’s 
proposed technical approach into the final contract award. 
2.1 Ant/Roach Treatment (Objective #1) 
2.1.1 Scheduled Biannual Treatment. The contractor shall perform full interior and exterior 
pest control treatments for all DoWEA Fort Campbell facilities twice per year. The first 
application shall be conducted during Summer Break prior to the start of the new school year, 
and the second application shall be completed during Winter Break. Each treatment shall include 
all interior areas of the facilities and extend up to 30 feet from the exterior perimeter to ensure 
effective perimeter protection. 
2.1.2 Coordinating and Scheduling. The contractor shall coordinate all treatment schedules 
with the Contracting Officer’s Representative (COR) to ensure minimal disruption to school 
operations. Advance notice of service dates and the expected duration of work shall be provided 
to the COR to facilitate proper planning and communication with school leadership. 
2.1.3 Follow- Up Services. The contractor shall monitor all treated areas for continued pest 
activity following each scheduled application. If pest activity persists, the contractor shall re-
service the affected areas at no additional cost to the government to ensure effective resolution. 
All follow-up actions must be thoroughly documented and reported to the Contracting Officer’s 
Representative (COR) to maintain transparency and accountability in service delivery.

school breaks—at no additional cost to the Government, provided it is coordinated with and 
approved by the Contracting Officer’s Representative (COR). Normal school operating hours are

caution must be exercised when working inside buildings to ensure safety and minimize 
disruption. No work shall be performed on federal holidays unless specifically authorized by the

3.3 Security Constraints 
All contractor personnel must obtain and carry valid base access credentials and always wear 
company-issued identification badges while on site. These badges must include a recent photo, 
the employee’s name, company name, and expiration date, and must be visible while on 
government premises. Contractors are required to comply with DoWEA visitor policies, 
including signing in daily at each school where work is performed during school hours. 
Services under Section 2.1 shall be performed exclusively during Summer Break and Winter 
Break periods when students are not present in school facilities. If work is required to be 
performed while school is in session and in the presence of students or performance falls outside 
the scope established in this contract, the contractor shall ensure that all contractor personnel 
comply with the background investigation requirements set forth in DoD Manual 1402.05. 
Contractor personnel shall not perform work during normal instructional hours unless expressly 
authorized in writing by the Contracting Officer’s Representative (COR). 
Personnel must maintain professional conduct and appearance, avoiding any behavior that could 
be considered disruptive, inappropriate, or unsafe. This includes refraining from verbal or 
physical contact with students or staff, avoiding offensive language or gestures, and adhering to 
dress codes appropriate for a pre-kindergarten through 12th grade school environment. 
The Government will provide the contractor with keys necessary to access designated facilities. 
All keys must be signed out and tracked by the contractor, who shall develop and maintain a Key 
Control Plan outlining procedures for secure handling, storage, and accountability. In the event 
of a lost or stolen key, the contractor must immediately notify the Contracting Officer’s

3.4 Safety Constraints 
Contractor personnel must follow all installation regulations regarding fire safety, environmental 
protection, sanitation, and security. Vehicles must be parked only in designated areas and must 
not block fire lanes or occupy spaces reserved for government or school staff. All pesticides must 
be EPA- and state-registered, securely stored off government property or in locked compartments 
within contractor vehicles, and applied only by certified personnel. Mixing of pesticides on 
government property is strictly prohibited. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) must be 
provided and used as required by pesticide labels and safety data sheets (SDS). 
In the event of a pesticide spill or other hazardous incident, the contractor must immediately 
notify the COR and relevant base authorities and is financially responsible for cleanup and 
decontamination. The contractor must also ensure that all buildings, gates, and fences are 
properly secured upon departure and that no unauthorized personnel are granted access. Any 
damage caused to government property must be reported within 24 hours and repaired or 
replaced at no cost to the government. Additionally, contractor personnel must comply with all 
Force Protection Condition (FPCON) protocols and be prepared to adjust operations based on 
changing threat levels as directed by the installation commander or COR. 
3.5 Personnel and Certification Constraints 
The contractor shall provide a sufficient number of experienced and qualified personnel to ensure 
high-quality performance of all services required under this contract. All personnel must be 
trained in Integrated Pest Management (IPM) principles and practices and must adhere to a 
written IPM policy outlining the contractor’s objectives. The contractor shall designate a 
Program Manager (PM) who will serve as the primary point of contact with the Government 
during normal working hours. The PM must be capable of conducting technical and non-
technical discussions and translating them into clear, grammatically correct documentation. An 
alternate shall be designated to act in the PM’s absence, with full authority to make decisions on 
behalf of the contractor. On-site supervisors and the designated Program Manager (PM) must be 
fluent in English to ensure clear communication with Government personnel and to support 
accurate documentation. 
Contractor personnel must maintain up-to-date certifications and comply with all federal, state, 
local, DoD, and installation-specific health and safety regulations. All pesticide applicators must 
possess current commercial certification in the appropriate EPA-approved state categories.

3.6 Vehicle and Equipment Constraints 
The contractor shall provide all vehicles, tools, equipment, and supplies necessary to fulfill the 
requirements of this contract. All equipment must be used in accordance with manufacturer 
recommendations and meet all applicable safety standards. Electrical equipment must be UL-
approved and compatible with existing building circuits. The contractor is responsible for 
ensuring that no equipment exceeds the power capacity of the facility’s electrical infrastructure. 
All pesticide application and pest control equipment must be clearly labeled with the company 
name, a point of contact, and phone number. Equipment must be in good working condition, free 
of leaks or deterioration, and calibrated to apply pesticides according to label specifications. 
Calibration records must be maintained and made available to the COR upon request. 
Vehicles used for pest control operations must meet Fort Campbell installation standards and 
display the contractor’s company name. These vehicles must include lockable storage 
compartments for pesticides, which must be secured when not in use. Vehicles shall not be used 
for any purpose other than pest control operations under this contract and must be equipped with 
a fire extinguisher, emergency wash water, portable eye wash station, and a spill and 
decontamination kit. All pesticides must be stored in a locked compartment separate from the 
vehicle cab. Vehicles are subject to inspection and must comply with all base access procedures, 
including registration with the Provost Marshal’s Office. The contractor is responsible for 
ensuring timely access to the installation and will not be reimbursed for delays at security 
checkpoints.

6.0 Definitions – Section 
Term Definition 
Contracting Officer (KO) The Government official with the authority to 
enter into, administer, and terminate contracts. 
The KO is the only individual authorized to 
make changes to the contract. 
Contracting Officer’s Representative (COR) An individual designated in writing by the KO 
to perform specific technical and 
administrative functions related to contract 
oversight. The COR monitors contractor 
performance, ensures compliance with the 
PWS, and serves as the primary point of 
contact for coordination. 
Integrated Pest Management (IPM) A comprehensive approach to pest control 
that combines biological, cultural, physical, 
and chemical tools in a way that minimizes 
economic, health, and environmental risks. 
Pest Management Plan (PMP) A written plan outlining the procedures, 
schedules, and methods used to implement 
IPM strategies across DoWEA Fort Campbell 
facilities. 
Critter Control (CC) A subset of pest management services focused 
on the humane removal and control of wildlife 
such as snakes, skunks, raccoons, and other 
animals that pose health or safety risks. 
Acceptable Quality Level (AQL) The minimum performance standard or 
margin of error allowed under the contract. 
AQLs are used to measure contractor 
compliance and service quality. 
Service Report A written document submitted by the 
contractor detailing the date of service, 
location, type of pest activity, treatment 
performed, chemicals used, and any follow-up 
actions. 
EPA-Registered Pesticide A pesticide product that has been reviewed 
and approved by the Environmental 
Protection Agency for safe use according to 
label instructions. 
Medical Surveillance A health monitoring program for pesticide 
applicators that includes physical exams and 
cholinesterase level testing, as required by AR 
40-5 and 29 CFR 1910. 
Force Protection Condition (FPCON) A system used by the Department of Defense 
to describe and respond to varying levels of 
terrorist threats. Contractor operations may be 
adjusted based on current FPCON levels.

7.1 Quality Assurance Surveillance Plan (QASP) – Subsection 
The Government will utilize a Quality Assurance Surveillance Plan (QASP) to monitor 
contractor performance throughout the contract period. Surveillance methods include: 
- Direct Observation: On-site inspections during and after service delivery. 
- Document Review: Evaluation of service reports, certifications, and incident documentation. 
- Customer Feedback: Review of complaints and satisfaction surveys from school staff and 
administrators. 
- Random Inspections: Unannounced checks of treated areas to verify compliance. 
 
The QASP may be updated unilaterally by the Government to reflect changes in performance 
expectations or operational needs.
